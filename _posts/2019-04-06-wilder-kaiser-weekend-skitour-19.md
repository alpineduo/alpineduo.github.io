We did skitouring 2 weeks ago in Wilder Kaiser and enjoyed it very much. Therefore we decided to repeat it with friends again. This time 7 of us. Danka suddenly a day before departure got flu and had to stay home. The weather forecast was sunny, some clouds later on Sunday. No new snow reported in the area and stable conditions for few days already.

We arrived rigth after 9 am to the Griesner Alm parking lot. It was nearly full. We were surprised how many cars there. When we looked up to the forest and first saddle, we saw crowds going up. Spring season is here! We were afraid to queue often. It was not the case on the way to the hut, however at least once we got delayed due to skitourer slide. We arrived to the hut by lunchtime, it was closed and empty. We took some rest, waited for entire group to unite again. Sun was shining, no clouds around and people everywhere. Two skitourers joined us at the hut, we were 9 people total there for the weekend.

After the short break, we decided to try Kleines Torl. Kleines Torl is steep short gully. The snow was melting, our skins started to collect some snow. As we were ascending, it became obvious to all of us this is not ideal to complete and ski down for all of us. There were two options - either go up on foot or just ski down. We decided to turn around at the narrower section. It was a good call, some of our friends haven't skied much this season and this was a very good start to introduce them to steeper lines. The descent was good, all of us enjoyed it and looked forward for late lunch. After the food we were eager to do more skitouring around. Griesner kar got empty in the late afternoon, we were alone. We decided to go for sunset. We realized later to see the sunset we would need to climb to one of the saddles around. That was not a good idea, so we stayed in Griesner Kar and look for lines. It was already hard and icy. We stayed at Mittlekaiser and watched sun going down in the valley.

Sunday was the day to finish at least one skitour. We decided to go to Goinger Torl. As we were approaching the scharte, we noticed how many skitourers are there (question we asked ourselfs - where is the lift if there are so many people). It was fine though as it is quite wide in the beginning, people stop by in many places around and scatter themselfs around. The sun was shining to the scharte for some time already, snow became slushy. We decided to stop at the narrower section where we noticed first steps in the snow. Time to put our skis on our back and just walk up to the saddle. The walk up was easy as tracks were already done. Some skitourers were still sknning up. It was harder due to the conditions but managable. However I enjoyed walking up as it was much faster and safer for entire group. Not all of us reached the top, some stayed down. We stared to the steep narrower gully and started having doubts about how to ski it safely. I went first. It was very good, snow was not that heavy as it felt on the way up and not many people going up at that time. All of us made it safely down without any fall. I was satisfied with the day and it was just the beginning. Some of us decided to ski down bit more (not traverse back to the hut). We got back to the hut by lunch time. Got some rest, packed ourselfs, cleaned the hut and left to ski down. Two of us decide to skin up a bit more to do more lines. The rest of us went down. We all met at Griesner Kar later in the afternoon. It was awesome weekend with friends, good conditions, perfect weather. Repeat anytime.
